Kepler-1649c -- Second Earth?

: Admin   : Discovery Date: 15 April 2020

The exoplanet was identified as a rocky planet by NASA and is very similar to Earth in terms of size, with a radius of 1.06 times that of Earth. Kepler-1649c takes only 19.5 Earth days to orbit its host star Kepler-1649, an M-Type red dwarf. It orbits within the habitable zone of its star system.Very little is known of Kepler-1649c's climate. It receives fully 75% of the light from its host star that Earth receives from the Sun.

TOI 1338 -- Boy 17 Years Old Founded?

: Admin   : Discovery Date: 6 January 2020

TOI 1338 is a binary star system located in the constellation Pictor, about 1,300 light-years from Earth. It was discovered by TESS and is orbited by a circumbinary planet known as TOI 1338 b. The two stars with masses of about 1.2 and 0.325 M revolve around each other every 14.6 days. The planet was found by a high school student, who joined the Goddard Space Flight Center as a summer intern. He looked through light curves that were flagged as eclipsing binaries by volunteers of the Planet Hunters citizen science project.
